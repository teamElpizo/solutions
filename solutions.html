Ask SOP

Our first solution aims to address the issue that some of our paramedics might need more information regarding how to deal with cases at scene and they need it fast. We therefore present Ask SOP, an application that has a chatbot equipped with all the protocols for medical, trauma and HAZMAT emergencies. On top of this, we also equipped the chatbot with data about Emergency Medical Service operating procedures for those who hold secondary appointments in the force. Check 3

Check 3 is a phone/online application that helps those working in the ambulances to keep track of what materials they have used during their calls or during training. It even has a Drawing List that can be shared with other colleagues through SMS, WhatsApp, Instagram or Facebook.

  • ZOLL

    An BP monitor, SP02 sensor, AED machine, ECG reader and message transmittor all built into one compact machine.

    The trauma bag is the supply bag filled with everything that you may need during calls.

Manning365

To run an emergency response force for all 31,536,000 seconds of the year, a roster has to be put up. Currently, there are many different roster ICs, each in charge of the rosters for regulars, NSFs and ORNSmen. Every month, they have to painstakingly calculate the hours each personnel has worked to allocate meal allowances accordingly, as well as take note of their shift patterns, swaps between Rotas or even stations, and VLs or MCs left. Hence there is a need for multiple roster ICs to tend to these administrative matters on top of their main duty to respond to emergency calls.
